## Best Early-Game Weapons

Getting a strong weapon early makes a huge difference in Crimson Desert. Here are the weapons you should aim for in the first 15 hours.

---

## Top Early Weapons

### 1. Hwando (Best Overall Early Weapon)

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Attack** | High |
| **Speed** | Medium-Fast |
| **Scaling** | STR / DEX |
| **How to Get** | Found in a chest in Hernand region |
| **Unique** | Can be infused with Abyss elements |

The **Hwando** is widely considered the best early-game weapon because:
- Accessible very early (Chapter 1-2)
- High base damage
- Accepts Abyss Gear upgrades
- Good moveset for most enemy types

### 2. Sustained Supply Arrow (Best Ranged)

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Type** | Unlimited arrows |
| **Damage** | Medium |
| **How to Get** | Talk to Lumiel in Pororin Village |
| **Unique** | Never runs out of ammo |

This is essentially an **infinite arrow supply**. Get it from the **Research Director** in Pororin Village. It's a quest reward for the Forest Guardians questline.

> **Priority:** Get this ASAP. Unlimited arrows means you can use ranged attacks freely without worrying about resource management.

### 3. Mining Knuckledrill (Utility Weapon)

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Type** | Mining tool / weapon |
| **How to Get** | Defeat Marni's Excavatron OR Bleed Bandits |
| **Unique** | Required for mining ore nodes |

Not a combat weapon, but essential for progression. You need this to mine ores for weapon and armor upgrades.

## Refinement (Upgrade) System

Weapons can be upgraded at any **blacksmith** using gathered materials.

### How to Refine

1. Visit a blacksmith in any major settlement
2. Select the weapon to upgrade
3. Pay the material cost + Silver fee
4. Each refinement level increases ATK

### Materials Needed

| Material | Source | Used For |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Iron Ore | Mining nodes (need Knuckledrill) | +1 to +3 |
| Steel Ingot | Blacksmith forge (2 Iron Ore) | +4 to +6 |
| Moonstone | Boss drops, Ancient Ruins | +7 to +9 |
| Dragon Scale | End-game bosses only | +10+ |

### Refinement Tips

- **Always upgrade your main weapon first** — damage matters most
- **Don't spread upgrades** across multiple weapons early
- You can change **elemental infusions** at enchantment stations
- Some weapons have **hidden upgrade paths** unlocked by special quests

## Abyss Gear System

Abyss Gear are special items that provide passive bonuses. They can be equipped to enhance your build.

### How to Get Abyss Gear

1. **Abyss Artifacts** — found in dungeons and hidden areas
2. **Abyss Nexus** — interact with them to unlock bonuses
3. **Boss drops** — some bosses drop unique Abyss items
4. **Quest rewards** — certain questlines grant Abyss Gear

### Best Early Abyss Gear

| Gear | Effect | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| Axiom Force | Combat tether, gap closer | Skill unlock |
| Abyss Core (ATK) | +5% attack | Sanctum loot |
| Abyss Core (DEF) | +5% defense | Sanctum loot |
| Abyss Core (STA) | +10% stamina | Ancient Ruins |

### How to Equip Abyss Cores

1. Open the **Abyss menu** from the pause screen
2. Navigate to **Core Allocation**
3. Drag cores into available slots
4. Each core provides a passive bonus
5. You can swap cores at any time (no cost)

> **Tip:** Prioritize **stamina cores** early — they provide the biggest quality-of-life improvement.

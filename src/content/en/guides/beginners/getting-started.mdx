## Welcome to Crimson Desert

Crimson Desert is an open-world action RPG developed by Pearl Abyss, set on the war-torn continent of **Pywel**. You play as **Kliff**, leader of the Greymane mercenaries, caught in a struggle against the Black Bears and the supernatural force known as the **Abyss**. The game features a deep combat system, vast exploration, and a story spanning dozens of hours.

This guide covers everything a new player needs to know — from basic controls to advanced mechanics that the tutorial only hints at.

> **Tip:** If you're on PC, try both input methods. The combat system feels slightly different on each, and some players find parry timing easier on controller while aiming bows is more natural with mouse.

### Display and Performance Settings

Before starting, adjust these settings for the best experience:

1. **Camera Sensitivity** — Lower it slightly from default. Precise camera control matters for parrying.
2. **Motion Blur** — Consider turning it off. It can obscure enemy attack animations.
3. **HUD Opacity** — Keep it at default; you'll need to see stamina and health clearly.
4. **Performance Mode** — If on console, choose Performance Mode for smoother combat at 60fps over graphical fidelity.

## Understanding the HUD

The heads-up display in Crimson Desert contains several critical elements:

### Health and Stamina Bars

- **Health Bar** (top left) — Your hit points. Depletes when hit. Restore with potions or food.
- **Stamina Bar** (below health) — Used for dodging, sprinting, heavy attacks, and blocking. Regenerates automatically but depletes fast in combat.
- **Abyss Gauge** (below stamina) — Fills as you deal and receive damage. When full, enables Abyss Powers.

> **Critical:** Running out of stamina mid-combat is the number one cause of death for new players. Always keep at least one dodge's worth of stamina in reserve.

### Minimap and Waypoints

- The **minimap** (bottom right) shows nearby enemies, NPCs, quest markers, and points of interest.
- **Gold markers** indicate main story objectives.
- **Blue markers** show side quests and NPC requests.
- **Red pulses** indicate nearby danger (elite enemies, bosses).

### Quick Access Bar

- **Bottom center** shows your equipped items and quick-use slots.
- Assign health potions to the first slot for fast access during combat.
- You can cycle through items with the mouse wheel or d-pad.

## First Steps: The Prologue

When you first start the game, you'll experience the **Dead of Night** prologue. This sequence serves as both story introduction and tutorial.

### What the Prologue Teaches

1. **Basic combat** — light attacks, heavy attacks, blocking
2. **Parry timing** — the most important mechanic in the game
3. **Movement and dodging** — essential for the chaotic battlefield
4. **Interactive cutscenes** — timed button prompts during key moments

### Prologue Tips

- **Talk to every Greymane member** in camp before the ambush begins. Several unlock side quests later in the game that you cannot access otherwise.
- **Collect all loot** during the escape sequence. You cannot return to the prologue area later.
- **Do not skip the combat tutorials**. Even if you're an experienced action RPG player, Crimson Desert's timing windows are unique and require practice.
- Pay special attention during the **Unknown Space** section — it introduces the Abyss, lantern mechanics, and your first Abyss Artifacts.

## Combat Fundamentals

Crimson Desert features a deep action combat system that rewards timing, positioning, and resource management over button-mashing. Master these basics early.

### Attack Types

| Attack | Speed | Damage | Stagger | Stamina Cost |
|--------|-------|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Light Attack** | Fast | Low | Low | Low |
| **Heavy Attack** | Slow | High | High | Medium |
| **Charged Attack** | Very Slow | Very High | Very High | High |
| **Combo Finisher** | Medium | High | Medium | Medium |

### Core Mechanics

1. **Light attacks** are fast but deal less damage. Use them to build combos and apply pressure.
2. **Heavy attacks** are slower but stagger enemies, opening them up for follow-up hits.
3. **Dodging** has invincibility frames (i-frames). Time them well to phase through enemy attacks.
4. **Parrying** at the right moment opens enemies up for critical hits and deals posture damage.
5. **Grab attacks** bypass shields and armor — invest in the Wrestling tree early.

### The Posture System

Every enemy (including bosses) has an invisible **posture bar** that fills when you:
- Land attacks (especially heavy attacks)
- Successfully parry their strikes
- Use Shield Bash or Grab attacks

When posture breaks, the enemy is **stunned** for several seconds, allowing a free combo. This is the key to defeating tougher enemies efficiently.

> **Tip:** Mixing light attacks into heavy attack combos builds posture faster than using either type alone. Try: Light x2, Heavy, Light x3, Heavy for a basic but effective pattern.

### Enemy Awareness System

Enemies in Crimson Desert have awareness states:

| State | Indicator | Behavior |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Unaware** | No marker | Patrolling, can be ambushed |
| **Suspicious** | Yellow "?" | Investigating, not hostile yet |
| **Alert** | Red "!" | Actively attacking |
| **Searching** | Red "?" | Lost sight of you, patrolling |

Use **stealth** (crouch in tall grass, use distractions) to pick off isolated enemies before groups can overwhelm you.

## Understanding the UI Menus

### Inventory

Your inventory is divided into several tabs:

- **Equipment** — Weapons, armor, and accessories
- **Consumables** — Potions, food, bombs, and buffs
- **Materials** — Crafting and upgrade materials
- **Key Items** — Quest items and special objects
- **Abyss Gear** — Abyss Cores and Artifacts

### Skill Tree

The skill system has multiple trees (see our [Best Skills Guide](/en/guides/skills/best-skills-early-game) for full details):

| Tree | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Keen Senses** | Dodge, parry, counters |
| **Sword Mastery** | Sword combos and techniques |
| **Shield Arts** | Bash, charge, defense |
| **Wrestling** | Grabs, throws, crowd control |
| **Archery** | Bow skills, ranged attacks |
| **Abyss Powers** | Supernatural abilities |

> **Recommendation:** Unlock **Keen Senses I** and **Forward Slash** as your first two skills. These two alone will dramatically improve your early combat effectiveness.

### Map and Fast Travel

- The map starts covered in **fog of war**. Walk through areas to reveal them.
- **Abyss Nexus** points serve as fast travel locations. Activate every one you find.
- Use high vantage points and bells to reveal larger sections of the map.

## Essential Systems to Learn Early

### Contribution Points

As you complete quests, discover locations, and defeat enemies, you earn **Contribution Points** within each region. These unlock:

- Regional rewards (armor pieces, weapons, crafting materials)
- Camp upgrades
- NPC discounts

Focus on Hernand first — the Contribution rewards here include some of the best early-game gear.

### Camp Management

Your camp serves as a home base. Key features:

1. **Cooking station** — Prepare food for combat buffs
2. **Crafting** — Create potions, bombs, and upgrade materials
3. **Rest** — Recover health and set time of day
4. **Companions** — Talk to recruited allies for new quests and lore

### Abyss Gear and Cores

Abyss Gear provides passive bonuses. You'll find Abyss Artifacts throughout the world in dungeons, hidden areas, and as boss drops. Equip them in the **Abyss Menu** under Core Allocation.

Priority for early Abyss Cores:
1. **Stamina** (+10% stamina) — biggest quality-of-life improvement
2. **Attack** (+5% attack) — helps with combat
3. **Defense** (+5% defense) — survival buffer

## What to Do First: Priority Checklist

Use this checklist for your first 5-10 hours:

### Immediate (Hours 0-2)

- [ ] Complete the prologue (do not skip tutorials)
- [ ] Arrive in Hernand and speak to all key NPCs
- [ ] Unlock your first skill (Keen Senses I or Forward Slash)
- [ ] Find and equip a better weapon than your starter gear

### Early Priority (Hours 2-5)

- [ ] Visit **Sanctum of Temperance** for Odeck's Protector Plate Boots
- [ ] Visit **Sanctum of Penitence** for Blackwing Leather Gloves
- [ ] Obtain the **Hwando** weapon from Hernand region
- [ ] Activate all fast travel points (Abyss Nexus) in Hernand
- [ ] Complete the **Trials of Kindness** quest for Contribution Points

### Early-Mid (Hours 5-10)

- [ ] Unlock **Keen Senses II** (Perfect Dodge) — this is a game-changer
- [ ] Get a pet for auto-looting (huge quality of life upgrade)
- [ ] Obtain the **Mining Knuckledrill** for ore gathering
- [ ] Complete the **Polar Opposites** sub quest (introduces elements)
- [ ] Attempt the **Hernand Bank Heist** for early Silver
- [ ] Tame your first horse for faster exploration

## Common New Player Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls that trip up many new players:

1. **Ignoring stamina management** — always keep a reserve for dodging
2. **Skipping side quests** — Crimson Desert scales with your gear, not just level
3. **Not upgrading weapons** — visit the blacksmith regularly; a +3 weapon feels dramatically different
4. **Forgetting to cook food** — food buffs provide significant combat advantages
5. **Neglecting the parry system** — enemies get much harder if you only dodge
6. **Fast-traveling everywhere** — you miss hidden loot, encounters, and lore
7. **Spreading skill points too thin** — focus on one or two trees first

> **Final advice:** Take your time. Crimson Desert is not a game that rewards rushing. Explore, experiment with combat, talk to NPCs, and enjoy the world of Pywel. The journey of Kliff and the Greymanes is worth savoring.
